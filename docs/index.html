Tools & Workflow

End-to-end analytics pipeline from raw data to published dashboard

01

Data Collection

Employee attendance data collected in Microsoft Excel with separate sheets for each month.

02

Data Transformation

Cleaned, unpivoted, and normalized using Power Query. Merged monthly sheets into one table.

03

Measure Creation

Built core and percentage measures using DAX — Presence %, WFH %, SL %, and Total Working Days.

04

Dashboard Design

Designed interactive visuals in Power BI Desktop — trend charts, KPI cards, slicers, and tables.

Key Insights

Data-driven findings from the attendance analysis

01

Strong Workforce Presence

At 91.55%, the organization maintains healthy in-office attendance across the three-month period.

02

Moderate Hybrid Adoption

WFH at 11.15% suggests a balanced hybrid model — remote work is available but not overused.

03

Minimal Sick Leave

SL at just 1.08% indicates good employee well-being and low unplanned absences.

04

Weekday Patterns

Monday (92.66%) shows strong start-of-week attendance while Friday (90.08%) sees a slight dip.

05

Employee Consistency

Most employees maintain above 90% presence, with a few outliers showing higher WFH or SL rates.

06

Stable Trends

Trend charts confirm attendance remained consistent across April, May, and June 2022 with no major disruptions.
